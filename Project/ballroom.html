History

Ballroom dancing can be traced back to 16th century Europe. Because the Europeans made partner dancing so distinguished, different forms of ballroom blossomed from different parts of the world. The waltz came from Vienna, the Merengue may have originated from Africa but is very popular in Caribbean and South American Countries. There is also the Tango, coming from Argentina and the Foxtrot stemming from the United States. These are only a few styles of ballroom, many of them the same name but differ in style or technique due to the vast travel they have made around the world.

Benefits

Ballroom helps physically, mentally, and socially. Physically, ballroom helps with flexibility, strength in the legs and abdomen, improves heart health, and POSTURE! Ballroom is all about the posture.
Mentally it helps with stress, focus, memorization, self-esteem, and emotions. Socially, ballroom helps with communication, teamwork, community, and mindfullness.
